Tea

Tea (scientifically known as Camellia sinensis) is one of the most popular beverages in the world and is consumed daily by millions of people. It is an evergreen plant mainly grown in tropical and subtropical regions with high rainfall and humid climate conditions. Tea plants are usually maintained as short bushes for easy harvesting, and their fresh green leaves are carefully plucked to produce different varieties of tea such as black tea, green tea, and oolong tea. The crop generally grows well in hilly areas and requires warm temperatures, rich soil, and regular rainfall for healthy growth.

Countries like India, China, Sri Lanka, and Kenya are among the major producers of tea, with India being famous for regions such as Assam and Darjeeling.

Tea is mainly used as a refreshing drink and is enjoyed in different forms across cultures. The cultivation of tea involves planting saplings in fertile, well-drained soil, regular pruning of bushes, careful plucking of young leaves, and processing them through drying and fermentation methods. Tea plants require cool to warm temperatures, abundant rainfall, and slightly acidic soil for better production.
